Brand System — Digital Editions

First Edition

One coherent identity across the museum, the course map, and everything descended from Knuth's masterwork — drawn directly from the covers themselves.

§ 1

Brand essence

The identity has one job: make a screen feel like an object you'd find on a shelf. Every choice should read as bibliographic — a scholarly press setting a canonical text — not as a web app dressed in serifs.

Three principles hold it together. Restraint: the page is quiet parchment and ink; colour appears only where it means something. Provenance: the colour, the banner, the volume block, and the tracked author line are lifted from the physical covers, so the digital work inherits the authority of the print. Precision: like the mathematics it presents, the typography is measured — a fixed scale, hairline rules, and contrast that never drops below legibility.

The through-line. Plum is the master brand. Each volume then carries its own cover ink, so a reader always knows which of the four wings — which of the four books — they are standing in.

On fidelity. The printed covers are set in a Modern serif (high stroke contrast, vertical stress). Cormorant is a warmer old-style face and a perfectly good stand-in; if you ever want the display to exactly echo the print, that's the single lever to pull. Body and mono are already right.

Signature devices

The recognizable furniture of the covers. Use them consistently and any page instantly reads as part of the work.

§ 7

Applying it to the properties

The museum

Each exhibit carries its wing's volume ink on the cover and prose accents (drop cap, section numbers, pull quotes, links); neutrals are warmed to ink #1F1B16 and muted #6B5D4F. The atrium keeps plum as master while tinting each wing section with its ink, and opens with the classic-work banner.

The course map (Docusaurus / Infima)

Unify creamOne parchment everywhere: --ifm-background-color:#F4EDDD; cards on ivory #FBF7EA.
Warm the greysOverride --ifm-menu-color from Infima's cool #606770 to #6B5D4F.
Fix linksPlum links sit at 1.36:1 against body text — underline .markdown a, always.
AccentAdd the volume inks as --v1 … --v4c so shelves, cards, and doc formulas can be colour-keyed to their book.
§ 8

Do & don't

Do

  • Let plum lead and volume inks orient — one accent per view.
  • Reach for the darkened amber-ink and deep-teal wherever text is involved.
  • Keep parchment and ink warm; hairline rules over boxes.
  • Set edition lines and the tracked author line — the bibliographic tells.
  • Underline links, always.

Don't

  • Never set orange, teal, or gold as body text on parchment.
  • Don't run several different creams across the properties.
  • Don't leave cool Docusaurus greys against a warm page.
  • Don't reproduce the publisher's trademark — use your own colophon.
  • Don't let colour be the only signal for a link or state.
